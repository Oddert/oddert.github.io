Bench and Branding Design for Sparda Bank

A project to design an icon bench for the German cooperative bank Sparda to use in their branches and also as a promotional item.

I undertook the brief set on Jovoto as a sort of experiment, to step out with my comfort zone and do the thing I vowed never to do; design a chair. The brief asked for a themed, general use bench to be used in branch which should encapsulate a new aspect of branding identity, being used at promotional events and on promotional material as well.
Sparda bank is a cooperative, with every member owning at least a small share, as a result it has a strong connection with its customers and location of origin, Stuttgart. In approaching this project, I decided that I wanted to project a warm, friendly attitude with reference to this location. Stuttgart is most associated with two things, the rolling hillsides, unusual for the region, and the automotive industry, being the home of Porsche and Mercedes-Benz. Both companies have elaborate HQ and factories attracting visitors, the sculpture outside the Porsche factory inspiring an aspect of the design.
In German, the word for ‘branch’ sounds very much like ‘bank’, inspiring the leg design which imitate tree branches, the Porsche sculpture and the idea of small parts holding up the collective whole all at once.
The main body is sloped at different levels for two reasons, the first being ergonomic accessibility, each ‘section’ being designed on anthropometric data for a particular user group to try to include as large a section of the population as possible. The different levels serve another function in that they, along with the back rest, make allusion to the sloping hills of Stuttgart. Light pine wood and encased live-end acrylic sheets give a light and inviting impression.

By Robyn Veitch
