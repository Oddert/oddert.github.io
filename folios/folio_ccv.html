CCV Interface Table

One of my earliest self-directed projects was to create an interactive computer interface.

The device was designed over the summer between fourth and fifth year of high school to use an open source software called Community Core Vison designed for this purpose.

The main interface for CCV, on the left is the raw infrared image, the right shows the filtered image and registered touch points.

The device utilises a standard webcam converted to pick up infrared, with the IR filter removed and a visible light filter (the end of a roll of film) in place. This camera picks up diffused light from a sheet of light diffusing polymethacrylate. The sheet Is flooded with IR light which diffuses downwards on the users touch, the software then detects touch points or clicks.

The final camera assembly after modification.

The assembly was designed to be aesthetic but functional, the less light that leaks in form the outside, the more sensitive the IR camera will be and the clearer the projected image will be.

The exploded CAD model, designed as a reference for the real-world build.

The final design was going to be built but I decided against it due to cost and storage concerns. The software and dimensioning was functioning as a proof of concept.
